Knesset’s data used to be a complete mess. IsraParlTweet1 is a vast database of Knesset quotes and MKs’ tweets from the past ~30 years. Accessing the data through a database doesn’t necessarily reveal trends, consistent opinions, or meaningful insights – you must sift through an enormous amount of textual information that isn’t easy to analyze.

Example questions we want to answer include:

  strongly
  con strongly
pro
time

Has an individual MK’s stance remained stable, shifted over time, or never been consistent to begin with?

positivity time

Have political blocs maintained their rhetoric over time? Have they actually become more extreme, as commonly believed? Have they all shifted “rightward”? Or have we simply become more pluralistic?

positivity time

Is there a correlation between two given topics?

Knessight uses advanced AI tools, analyzes trends and opinions for you, and provides you with easy access to them.

To extract relevant data for a specific topic concerning an individual Member of Knesset from quotations, we follow this 4-stage approach:

Discussion Text – Retrieving all records from the IsraParlTweet database that were spoken by the MK.

Filtering – Using a lightweight LLM model that assigns a score between 1 and 5 based on how relevant each quote is to the topic, and filtering out sentences that scored below 4.

Scoring – Applying a more advanced LLM model to assign a score between 1 and 10, indicating how positive each relevant quote is toward the topic.

Presentation for Review – Storing the results in an accessible user database and providing an inviting and interactive UI for viewing the analysis.
